EARLY ADULTHOOD

1

Introduction

Early adulthood (ages 19–40) is a stage of independence, career growth, and increased responsibilities. It is an important period for building relationships, achieving financial stability, and making life decisions that shape the future.

2

Physical & Cognitive Development

Physical and cognitive development in early adulthood is marked by peak health, strength, and brain maturity. Individuals develop better judgment, problem-solving, logical thinking, and decision-making skills, although physical abilities gradually decline over time.

3

Emotional & Social Development

Emotional and social development refers to how a person learns to understand emotions, build relationships, and interact with others during early adulthood.

4

Development Tasks & Responsibilities

During early adulthood, people focus on building careers, becoming financially independent, balancing work and personal life, supporting their families, and achieving future goals.

5

CONCLUSIONS

Early adulthood (ages 19–40) is a stage of growth and independence. It brings challenges such as work stress, finances, and balancing responsibilities, which can be managed through good planning, self-care, and support from others.
